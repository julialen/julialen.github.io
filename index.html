About Me

I am a METEOR postdoc at MIT working with Henry Corrigan-Gibbs. My research interests are broadly in applied cryptography and computer security.

Starting in July 2025, I will be an assistant professor of computer science at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. I'm recruiting Ph.D. students at UNC Chapel Hill for Fall 2025. If you're interested, list me as a potential faculty member in your application.

I completed my Ph.D. in computer science at Cornell University, where I was advised by Tom Ristenpart. I graduated from UC San Diego in June 2018 with a B.S. in Computer Science, where I was fortunate to work with Mihir Bellare. During my Ph.D., I was also fortunate to intern at Zoom with Antonio Marcedone and at Microsoft Research Redmond with Esha Ghosh and Melissa Chase.
